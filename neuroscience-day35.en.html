Neuroplasticity

"The brain can change" — yes, but it isn't free. Every reshaping squeezes something else.
Explanation

Neuroplasticity = the structural rewriting of neural circuits under experience. It isn't "want it, grow it." Hebb's full rule: "fire together, wire together; out of sync, lose the link." Cortical surface is zero-sum — strengthening one circuit prunes another.

Non-obvious points: ① Adult writing requires three co-occurring inputs — attention + prediction error + sleep consolidation. Miss one → experience leaves no trace (why "100 papers, zero growth"). ② Not practicing = pruning. Unused circuits get flagged "unimportant" by the same gate and dismantled. ③ Critical periods are temporary plasticity peaks, later sealed by perineuronal nets — sealing prevents catastrophic forgetting. Adult windows aren't gone, just locked; strong emotion + intensity + repeated exposure pries them open.

Practice pivot: before "learning X," ask whether the three conditions can co-occur. Reverse: did I feed an unwanted loop one less time today?

Adult plasticity = AND-gate · three co-occurring inputs, no substitutes
Classic Example

Maguire (2000) MRI on London cab drivers: after memorizing 25,000 streets, right posterior hippocampus enlarged, but anterior hippocampus shrank. Naked-eye proof that plasticity is budget allocation. Retired drivers slowly re-shrink — "use it or lose it" is physical fact, not metaphor.

BigCat Scenario

① You unlock your phone 80 times a day = training a "distraction circuit" 80× daily, all three conditions met. One hour of deep work can't compete — 1:80 dosage, not weak will. ② Correct learning posture: not a weekend 8-hour binge, but 3 weeks of daily 25 min with a real problem in hand + good sleep. ③ Use AI in reverse: don't let it explain (it flattens prediction error). Prompt it to refute you, give counterexamples. AI as explainer thins the brain; AI as challenger thickens it.

AI Prompts
English Prompt
I want to master [skill/domain], but keep [forgetting / stalling]. Diagnose with the three co-occurring conditions of adult plasticity: 1. Which of attention / prediction error / sleep is most offline? Cite evidence. 2. Design a 3-week cadence: daily dose, prediction-error source, sleep window. 3. Identify a circuit I'm reinforcing in the wrong direction; give one "feed it less once" action this week. 4. Am I using AI as explainer or as challenger? Suggest a concrete shift.

Predictive Processing

You don't "see" the world — you predict it, and let the senses correct the prediction. "Reality" is a sensory-constrained hallucination.
Explanation

Classical model: senses → brain → perception. Predictive Processing flips it: the brain is a prediction engine; the senses only carry "prediction error" upward. Friston's Free Energy Principle: minimize prediction error (≈ surprise) is the brain's single objective — perception, action, learning, emotion are all sub-cases.

Non-obvious points: ① Perception updates predictions to match data; action updates data to match predictions; learning updates priors. ② Emotion = interoceptive prediction. Anxiety = high-uncertainty internal-state prediction; why "just relax" fails and deep breathing / mindfulness works — they supply data to update the prediction. ③ Isomorphic to Bayesian inference, LLM next-token prediction, Yogācāra Buddhism, and the quantum observer effect. ④ Cost of seniority: strong priors compress incoming detail — what you "see at a glance" is also what you can no longer notice.

Practice pivot: write what surprised you today. Zero surprise = your model has frozen. AI use: let it refute you. A people-pleasing LLM is a cognitive sedative.

Hierarchical predictive coding · top-down predictions (blue) vs bottom-up errors (red)
Classic Example

Adelson's checker-shadow illusion: squares A and B have pixel-identical luminance, but the high-level prior "shadowed squares should be brighter" auto-corrects B. Cover the surrounding context — the illusion collapses. Top-down overrides bottom-up, naked-eye demonstration. Same reason "I saw it with my own eyes" claims need humility.

BigCat Scenario

① 15 years of code → reverse baggage on a new framework: "this is just X" is your strong prior compressing detail. Antidote: enter domains your templates don't cover (Buddhist shoshin). ② Anxiety's neural definition: high-uncertainty interoceptive prediction. Deep breathing works because it supplies high-quality interoceptive data, not because it's "calming." ③ An AI that always agrees = your world model stops updating. Make "refute me, find the weakest link" a default prompt.

AI Prompts
English Prompt
My current beliefs about [topic / decision / a person]: [list priors]. Run a predictive-processing diagnostic: 1. Which are strong priors? Where might priors be overwriting inconvenient data? 2. Generate 3 high-quality counterexamples / counter-questions / counter-data. Challenge me directly. 3. For each: is the fix to update the prior, or to change action so the data changes? 4. If emotion is involved, name the likely interoceptive prediction underneath it.

Default Mode Network (DMN)

The network most active when you're "doing nothing." It builds the thing you call "me" — and it's the easiest thing for modern life to starve.
Explanation

The DMN is most active when you're "doing nothing" (mPFC + PCC + angular gyrus + hippocampus), burning energy comparable to the task-positive network (TPN). "Rest" isn't idle — it's switching to another high-energy process: self-referential thought, autobiographical memory, mental time travel, mind-wandering.

Non-obvious points: ① The DMN constructs the self. Psilocybin and long-term meditation both reduce DMN connectivity, producing ego dissolution — "I" isn't a thing, it's a continuously running process. ② Double-edged: creative recombination and insight pre-computation happen here (the shower "aha"); rumination also lives here (a depression hallmark). ③ DMN and TPN are anti-correlated — healthy brains switch freely; unhealthy ones stick on one side. ④ Phones keep you task-positive — chronic DMN starvation → less creativity, weaker consolidation, blurry self.

Practice pivot: schedule audio-free DMN time — shower without audio, walk without podcast. Rule: phone in pocket ≠ DMN online. If themes loop "I should / if only…" — install a theme-switcher, not more DMN time.

DMN ⇌ TPN seesaw · always-on TPN = chronic DMN starvation
Classic Example

Carhart-Harris (2012, 2016) under IV psilocybin: DMN internal connectivity dropped sharply; subjective correlate = ego dissolution. Long-term meditators (> 10,000 hours) show analogous resting-state patterns. Among the hardest evidence the "self" is a brain process, not a substance — you don't have a self; the self is a process. Direct echo of Madhyamaka/Yogācāra millennia prior.

BigCat Scenario

① Your best architectural insights arrive in the shower or on a walk — not coincidence. Phone in the shower = killing the GC process. ② AI-era trap: a query while compiling, AI-chat while walking — surface "efficient," in fact zeroes the last DMN windows. "More AI = fewer new ideas" has a neural explanation. ③ For your child: let her be bored. Boredom isn't failure — it's the growth hormone of the DMN; a zoning-out child is self-building.

AI Prompts
English Prompt
My state this past week: [describe]. Run a DMN / TPN scan: 1. How many deliberate DMN sessions did I have (audio-free shower / walk / commute / staring)? Quantify. 2. Drifting into rumination — recurring theme and time of day? More DMN time, or a theme-switcher? 3. Am I filling every idle slot with AI / phone, starving the DMN? Name the 3 most dangerous fillers. 4. Prescribe this week: one DMN protection + one rumination-interrupt + one TPN load-shed.

Dopamine RPE

Dopamine is not the reward chemical — it's "reality minus expectation." Addiction, stalled learning, lost momentum: same equation.
Explanation

The most common dopamine misconception is "the pleasure molecule" — wrong. Schultz's VTA recordings proved dopamine encodes reward prediction error, δ = R − V, not reward itself. Predicted = no fire; surprise good = +; predicted-but-omitted = −. Mathematically identical to TD-learning — AlphaGo's update rule and your VTA run the same equation.

Non-obvious points: ① Predicted reward fires no dopamine — why month-2 of a promotion feels flat. Dopamine is numb to "level," sharp to "delta." ② Peak sits on the cue, not the reward. Berridge: wanting ≠ liking — addiction is wanting decoupled from liking. ③ Variable-ratio reward is the hardest to escape — δ never converges. Slots, feeds, shorts run this algorithm. Math problem, not willpower. ④ No δ = no learning. Perpetual "good job" and perpetual criticism are neurally equivalent — both zero the signal. ⑤ Buddhist tanha: predicted → next prior rises → δ chases anew. Liberation isn't destroying wanting, it's not being driven by it.

Practice pivot: can't stop = variable high δ (cut the cue); lost momentum = δ → 0 (manufacture visible prediction error). Effective feedback = specific + slightly above expectation + occasionally surprising.

RPE = R − V · variable-ratio keeps δ ≠ 0 = permanent hook
Classic Example

Schultz et al. (1993, 1997) trained monkeys: bell → juice. Post-training the firing peak migrated onto the bell. Critical test: withhold the juice — at predicted delivery time the VTA fires below baseline. V established, R missing, δ negative. This firing pattern was later identified as the δ term of TD-learning — computational neuroscience and reinforcement learning unified by one equation.

BigCat Scenario

① Short-video addiction's neural definition: variable-ratio + high-frequency cue. Antidote isn't "watch less" (will vs algorithm loses); it's cut the cue (uninstall, kill push, off home screen). ② Feedback to a child: perpetual "good job" = withholding the juice post-training; δ → 0, learning signal vanishes. Effective = specific + slightly above expectation + occasionally surprising ("how'd you think of that step?" beats 100 "good job"s). ③ Most dangerous AI trap: AI is variable-reward + salient cue + fast loop — perfect addictive form. Healthy use: batch queries; move the dopamine peak from cue ("let me ask") to outcome ("what I solved"). ④ All four models share δ: it's the dopamine gate of plasticity, the central signal of predictive processing, the trigger that flips DMN ⇌ TPN. One engine, four cross-sections.

AI Prompts
English Prompt
Analyze [an addictive loop / feedback that stopped teaching / incentive setup / a stalled project]. Run an RPE diagnostic: 1. Shape of δ — predictable low-δ (numb), variable high-δ (can't stop), or chronic negative δ (helpless)? 2. Peak on cue or outcome? Driven by "wanting" or "liking"? 3. Minimal redesign: R slightly above V, preserve some unpredictability, avoid the variable-ratio trap. 4. Is my "let me ask AI" impulse being reinforced into a near-addictive loop? Suggest a cue → outcome shift.

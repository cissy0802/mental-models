Neuroplasticity

"Cells that fire together wire together; cells out of sync, lose their link." — Hebb (1949) / Stent (1973)
Explanation

"The brain can rewire" is the most abused neuro-slogan. The truth: plasticity is budgeted and competitive. The full Hebbian rule has two halves — "fire together, wire together" AND "out of sync, lose the link." Strengthening one circuit silently prunes another.

Non-obvious points: ① Adult plasticity requires three co-occurring inputs — attention/salience (ACh and NE open the learning gate), prediction error (VTA dopamine flags "something to learn here"), and sleep consolidation (NREM slow waves + REM commit the day's weight updates). Miss one and experience leaves no trace. That's why you can read 100 papers and not improve. ② Isomorphic to LLM fine-tuning: catastrophic forgetting is real, but adult brains regularize via spaced + interleaved exposure. ③ You cannot add a habit without subtracting somewhere — cortical surface is zero-sum, energy is budgeted. Who you become is shaped not by what you do once but by what you repeatedly do — and repeatedly don't do. ④ Buddhist vāsanā (habit-energy) is STDP — spike-timing-dependent plasticity — at the metabolic level. Every passing thought etches a groove.

Practice pivot: translate "I want to learn X" into "over the next 3 weeks I'll give X daily attention + manufacture prediction errors + protect sleep." Only when all three co-occur does plasticity flow where you want. Otherwise you're just reinforcing the defaults.

Adult plasticity is an AND-gate — three co-occurring inputs, no substitutes
Classic Example

Maguire et al. (2000) scanned London taxi drivers who memorize "The Knowledge" (25,000 streets). Senior drivers showed significantly enlarged right posterior hippocampus, with size proportional to years driving — but their anterior hippocampus shrank. Cortical real estate is zero-sum: an expanding memory-map squeezed other functions. This was the first hard evidence of structural plasticity in adult human brains, and a direct demonstration of the plasticity budget.

BigCat Scenario

① You unlock your phone 80 times a day = you are deliberately training a "distraction circuit" 80 times daily. All three conditions met: high salience (red dot), strong prediction error (unpredictable content), and the circuit gets its last reinforcement right before sleep. One hour of deep work can't compete — not from lack of willpower, but a 1:80 dosage. ② Correct posture for learning a new framework: not a weekend 8-hour binge, but 3 consecutive weeks of daily 25 minutes with a specific problem in hand (attention + prediction error), then sleep well. That's what writes weights at the neural level. Weekend binges are muscle-memory illusion. ③ Use AI to manufacture prediction errors — it nodding at concepts you already know teaches nothing. Prompt it to refute you, supply counterexamples, give angles you've not seen. Turn AI into a high-density prediction-error generator — that's the real compute leverage for BigCat.

Predictive Processing

"The brain is a prediction machine; perception is controlled hallucination." — Andy Clark / Karl Friston
Explanation

Classical view: senses → brain → processing → perception. Predictive Processing flips it: the brain is a generative prediction engine; the senses only transmit how wrong the predictions were (prediction error). What you see ≈ your brain's best guess, sensory-corrected — Andy Clark calls it "controlled hallucination."

Non-obvious points: ① Friston's Free Energy Principle extends this to a unified objective for all brain activity — minimize prediction error (≈ minimize surprise). Perception, action, learning, emotion are all special cases: perception updates predictions to match data; action updates data to match predictions; learning updates priors. ② Emotions are interoceptive predictions. Anxiety = high uncertainty about internal state; depression = locked-in low-reward priors. Talk therapy and mindfulness both work by updating priors. ③ Fully isomorphic to Bayesian thinking: prior + likelihood → posterior. The brain runs approximate Bayesian inference constantly. ④ Same primitive as LLM next-token prediction — different substrate (carbon vs silicon). Transformer's success isn't coincidence; it approximates the brain's core operator. ⑤ Yogācāra Buddhism (1600 years ago) described "phenomena are mind-constructed" — the core of top-down perception. You don't see the world, you build it.

Practice pivot: keep a "surprise log" — surprise is the only place learning can happen. A day with zero surprise means either your model has frozen (cognitive ossification) or you've stopped exposing yourself to new data. "Boredom" and "surprise" are the two ends of prior quality.

Hierarchical predictive coding: top-down predictions (blue) vs bottom-up errors (red)
Classic Example

Adelson's "checker-shadow illusion": squares A and B have pixel-identical luminance on the screen, but your visual system, holding the prior "cylinder casts shadow → shadowed squares should be brighter than they look," up-corrects B. You don't see pixels, you see your brain's corrected best guess. Cover the surrounding context with paper exposing only A and B — the illusion collapses. You removed the context that produced the prior. This is top-down prediction overriding bottom-up data, visible to the naked eye.

BigCat Scenario

① The cost of seniority: you've coded for 15 years; one look at a new framework triggers "oh, it's just X" — strong priors compressing sensory input. The details a novice notices get auto-filled by your prediction engine. Antidote: deliberately enter domains where your templates don't apply (the Buddhist shoshin / beginner's mind targets exactly this). ② Debugging ≈ locating maximum prediction error. Senior engineering intuition is a finely-tuned prior about where failure lives. But the same prior, dragged into a new regime (LLMs/agents), becomes reverse baggage — "what should be" filters out "what actually is." ③ Neural definition of anxiety: a high-uncertainty interoceptive prediction. Deep breathing/mindfulness works because it supplies high-quality interoceptive data to update the prediction — not vague "relaxation." ④ The quantum-mechanical "observer effect" and predictive processing's "prior-shapes-perception" are structurally homologous — the observing process is inseparable from how the observed appears.

AI Prompts
English Prompt

Where might priors be overwriting inconvenient data? 2. Generate 3 high-quality prediction-error inputs — counterexamples, counter-questions, or counter-data I should sit with. 3. For each item: is the fix to update the prior, or to change action so the data changes? 4. If emotion is involved, name the likely interoceptive prediction underneath it.

Default Mode Network (DMN)

Raichle et al. (2001) — "The brain at 'rest' burns nearly as much glucose as the brain at work. It's not idle — it's running you."
Explanation

The DMN is the network most active when you're "doing nothing" (mPFC + PCC + angular gyrus + hippocampus). Functions: self-referential thought, autobiographical memory, mental time travel (recall + future imagination), theory of mind, mind-wandering. It consumes energy comparable to the task-positive network — "rest" is the brain switching to another high-energy process, not idling.

Non-obvious points: ① The DMN constructs the self. Carhart-Harris's fMRI work showed psilocybin reduces DMN internal connectivity, with subjective ego dissolution as the correlate. Long-term meditators exhibit analogous DMN modulation — "non-self" at the neural level is reduced DMN dominance. ② DMN is double-edged: creative recombination and insight pre-computation happen here (the shower "aha" is the DMN cross-shuffling the day's inputs); but so does rumination. A core neural feature of depression is over-active, sticky DMN. ③ Modern affliction: phones keep you always task-positive — chronic DMN starvation. Reduced creativity, weaker memory consolidation, blurry self-coherence. The "boredom crisis" isn't melodrama; it's DMN malnutrition. ④ Engineering analog: DMN ≈ background GC + index rebuild + offline learning. A cluster that never lets background processes run will OOM. ⑤ Buddhist link — "act without dwelling": the ideal isn't a DMN-off state (that's autobiography loss) but a DMN that's switchable — enter freely, exit freely.

Practice pivot: schedule DMN time deliberately — shower without audio, walk without podcast, commute staring out the window. Not waste — it's where insight is pre-computed and emotion gets integrated. Rule: phone in pocket ≠ DMN online (you're still expecting it to buzz; task-positive is on standby).

The two networks form an anti-correlated seesaw — always-on TPN = chronic DMN starvation
Classic Example

Carhart-Harris et al. (2012, 2016) administered IV psilocybin to volunteers under fMRI: DMN internal connectivity dropped sharply, with the subjective correlate of "ego dissolution / space-time deconstruction." Follow-up work showed long-term meditators (> 10,000 hours) display analogous resting-state DMN modularity reductions. This is among the hardest evidence that the "self" is a brain process, not a substance — you don't have a self; the self is a continuously running process. Switch it off, experience continues, but "I" disappears. This maps directly onto Madhyamaka and Yogācāra analyses of selfhood.

BigCat Scenario

① Your best architectural insights arrive in the shower or on a walk — not coincidence. The TPN collected the problems during the day; the DMN is doing the background recombination. Bring the phone into the shower = kill the GC process. ② Engineering analog: your brain is a cluster — TPN as stateless services (handling requests), DMN as stateful background (rebuilding indexes, compaction, ML jobs). A cluster that never runs background work eventually degrades every request into an N+1 slow path. ③ AI-era trap: filling every idle moment with an LLM query (during a compile, during a build) = zeroing what little DMN time remained. This is the most dangerous anti-pattern for a high-utilization user like BigCat. ④ Rumination warning: if the DMN keeps replaying "why didn't I…" or "if they meant X…" — you've entered depressive rumination. Mindfulness isn't stopping the DMN; it's installing a theme-switcher on it — once noticed, you can redirect to a wider-aperture topic. ⑤ For kids, the same rule: let her be bored. Don't fill every 5-minute gap with a tablet. Boredom is the growth hormone of a child's DMN.

English Prompt

Am I drifting into rumination — if so, what's the recurring theme and typical time of day? 3. Am I filling every idle slot with AI/phone, starving the DMN? 4. Prescribe the minimum this week: one DMN protection + one rumination-interrupt + one TPN load-shed.

Dopamine Reward Prediction Error (RPE)

Schultz, Dayan & Montague (1997) — "Dopamine doesn't signal reward. It signals the difference between expected and received reward."
Explanation

The most common misconception about dopamine is "the reward chemical" — wrong. Schultz's seminal VTA recordings in monkeys proved dopamine encodes reward prediction error, δ = R − V, not reward itself. Expected rewards barely fire; surprise rewards fire +; predicted-but-omitted rewards fire −. This is mathematically identical to TD-learning in reinforcement learning — AlphaGo's TD update and your VTA neurons execute the same equation.

Non-obvious points: ① Expected reward fires no dopamine — why month 2 of a promotion already feels flat, why romantic dinners feel like routine by year 5. The dopamine system is numb to "level" and sharp to "delta." ② The dopamine peak lives on the cue, not the reward. Anticipation before unlocking the app is stronger than the content itself. Berridge's "wanting ≠ liking" — two independent circuits. Addiction is the wanting system decoupled from the liking system. ③ Variable-ratio reward is the hardest to escape — the prediction error never converges to zero. Slot machines, social feeds, and short-form video all run on the same algorithm. Skinner knew it; the TikTok team knows it; your VTA neurons are programmed for it. ④ RPE is the engine of learning. No prediction error = no learning. A feedback system that always says "good job" zeroes the learning signal as much as one that always criticizes (which leaves V chronically above R, suppressing dopamine into learned helplessness). Effective feedback = slightly above prediction + not fully predictable. ⑤ Buddhist tanha (craving): the Buddha separated "wanting" from "satisfaction" 2500 years before Berridge. Suffering's root isn't lack — it's the structural never-quenching of the wanting system: once predicted, the prior rises, δ chases anew.

Practice pivot: don't chase rewards — engineer prediction errors. Feedback to your child (team, self): slightly above prediction + not fully predictable. The always-praising parent, the always-criticizing boss, and the always-flattering AI assistant are neurally equivalent — they all zero the learning signal.

RPE = R − V · expected = no learning; variable-ratio keeps δ ≠ 0 = permanent hook
Classic Example

Schultz et al. (1993, 1997) trained monkeys: bell → juice. Day 1: VTA neurons fire when juice arrives (unexpected good). Post-training: the firing peak migrates onto the bell — the cue now carries the prediction; the reward itself is expected. Critical test: after training is complete, withhold the juice — at the predicted delivery time the VTA fires below baseline. This is R − V visible to the eye: V is established but R never arrives, so δ is negative. The same firing pattern was later identified as the δ term of TD-learning — computational neuroscience and reinforcement learning unified by one equation.

BigCat Scenario

① Neural definition of TikTok/X/short-video addiction: variable-ratio reward + high-frequency cue. The next clip might be hilarious or boring — δ never converges; VTA runs forever. This isn't weak will; it's a precision attack on a vulnerability of your reward-learning algorithm. ② Real principle of feedback to a child or team: perpetual "good job" equals withholding the juice post-training — V has already learned to predict the praise, R is fully expected, δ → 0, the learning signal vanishes. She looks "praised," but neurally she gains nothing. Effective feedback = specific + slightly above her expectation + occasionally surprising. ③ Engineering homolog: if your reward function (OKRs/KPIs) is fully predictable, it stops driving learning in any agent — including teams and yourself. Inject unpredictable, high-signal feedback. This is what Karpathy means by "surprise is data." ④ Self-management: the thrill of starting something new decays fast — the project didn't get worse, V caught up with R, δ → 0. The fix is deliberately manufacturing visible prediction errors: difficulty ramps, surprise micro-challenges, new collaborators introducing new data. ⑤ Quantum-intuition link: posterior update is driven by the difference between measurement and prior — δ in the brain, likelihood ratio in Bayes. Three languages, one engine. ⑥ Buddhist tanha (craving): once a prediction is satisfied, the prior rises and δ chases anew — that's RPE's structural feature. Liberation isn't destroying the wanting system; it's not being driven by it. That's the neural definition of non-attachment.
